### statistical interference
- widespread tool for performing estimation and prediction in statistical inference
- statistical inference
	- methods for estimating and testing hypothesis about population characteristics based on info contained in a sample
- population
	- a collection of all elements of interest for a particular study
	- a parameter is a characteristic of a population, such that the mean number of customer service calls of all cell phone customers
- sample
	- sample is representative subset of population
	- if the sample characteristics deviate systematically from the population characteristic, statistical inference should not be applied 
- proportion
	- sample proportion is the statistic used to measure the unknown value of population proportion
- point estimation
	- use of single known value of a statistic to estimate the associated population parameter 

### how confident are we in our estimates?
- sampling error
	- distance between observed value of the point estimate and the unknown value of its target parameter
	- |statistic - parameter|
- point estimates have no measure of confidence in their accuracy

### confidence interval estimation
- consists of an interval of numbers produced by a point estimate 
- includes a confidence level specifying the probability the interval contains the population parameter
- gneral form of point estimate +- margin of error
	- the margin of error is a measure of precision and smaller value means greater precision
- can be applied to any target parameter
- usually used to estimate the population mean, population standard deviation, and population proportion of success

### hypothesis testing for the mean
- hypothesis testing evaluates claims about a population parameter using evidence from the sample
- null hypothesis - represents the assumed value
- alternative hypothesis - represents alternative claim about the value 
- two possible conclusions
	- reject H0 (null hypothesis)
